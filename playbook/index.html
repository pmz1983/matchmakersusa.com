A look at the Coach

You · Long-Term Intent
Matched with someone who fits everything I want on paper, but our texts feel flat. I keep wondering whether to push through and meet, or move on. How do I tell?
Coach
Two distinct things here. “Fits on paper” is Position — what they look like in your filter. “Texts feel flat” is Open — how the early signal is actually carrying. Position without a working Open is the most common misread in Long-Term Intent. Before you decide to push through or move on, run one calibrated in-person ask. Texts compress people; presence rebuilds them. If presence still reads flat, you’ve got real data.
▸ Why this works
Cites Chapter 3 — Position vs Open. Long-Term Intent under-weights medium (“texts” as proxy) and over-weights filter (“fits on paper”). The Coach reframes the question before answering it — every methodology citation is linked back to the Playbook chapter that built it.

A look at Script Builder

Your Inputs
Intent: Long-Term
Situation: We matched three weeks ago, had a good first date last Thursday, had to reschedule the second because their work trip moved. It’s been four days since we confirmed the new date. I want to send something that keeps the thread warm without making it feel like I’m checking up on them.
Their last message: “Totally — Wednesday works. Looking forward to it.”
Tone: Warm, direct
Length: Short
Script 1
Wednesday’s on the calendar — looking forward to it too. If you get back earlier than Tuesday and want to grab coffee before the real thing, I’m around. If not, I’ll see you then.
▸ Why this works
Uses Bridge + Optionality from Chapter 4. A reschedule creates a small warmth gap; a single forward-looking line + one low-stakes optional beat closes it without pressure. The “real thing” phrasing signals you’re taking the actual date seriously.
Script 2
Hope the trip’s going well. Not checking in — just thinking Wednesday’s going to be a good one. We never got to the part where I find out how you take your coffee.
▸ Why this works
Uses Callback to Specific Detail from Chapter 5. Naming a small specific (coffee) rather than a generic (“the date”) signals you remember the texture of the first date, not just that it happened.
Script 3
Quick thing — Wednesday I had us down for the place on Elm, but if you’d rather try the one with the patio I mentioned last time, I’ll flip it. Either way, looking forward.
▸ Why this works
Uses Invited Lead from Chapter 6. Giving them a small real decision (venue) 48 hours out keeps them anchored in the plan. Works best when the first date went well and you want to reinforce mutual investment without re-negotiating the date itself.

Example output. Your generated scripts are personalized to your Intent, your situation, and — with your consent — your actual match’s profile and conversation history.
